What is in it for you?

What this session gives you

  • Insight: AI can raise team throughput when workflows are redesigned, not just when tools are added
  • Evidence: customer-support field experiment showed +15% productivity with AI support
  • Caveat: task-level gains do not automatically become organization-level value
  • RIVM implication: start with 1 or 2 high-volume workflows and measure outcomes

Take into consideration:

  • Preparation time
  • Error rate
  • Reviewing & Fact-checking
  • Reproducibility

Why Vibe coding?

From expensive tools to flexible workflows

  • Insight: we are at a practical inflection point for coding agents in daily work
  • Evidence: +12.2% speed, +25.1% task completion, +33.9% quality inside capability frontier
  • Caveat: outside the frontier, correctness can drop significantly
  • RIVM implication: apply AI first to verifiable tasks with clear pass/fail checks

Summary

Key takeaways from this session

  • Start with one concrete workflow and define baseline metrics first
  • Use Copilot to accelerate drafting, but keep human review and fact-checking mandatory
  • Scale only when quality is stable and responsibilities are explicit
  • Treat AI as a controlled productivity tool, not as autonomous decision-maker

Practical conclusion

Pilot, measure, validate, and then expand in a controlled way.
